I started in 2013 because I was hooked on Temple Run and figured the name was a sign. Twelve years later I'm still shipping, mostly apps that connect people. Founding iOS at **Geneva** (acquired by **Bumble**), plus a handful of things I build because I can't not.

The whole arc is below. It reads best in order, so start at the top.

---

## I. The Build Log

*A chronological list of things I've made. Click any era to open it.*

<details>
<summary><b>2013-2017</b> &nbsp;·&nbsp; learning to ship</summary>

<br>

**2013.** Hooked on iPhone games: Temple Run, Flappy Bird. Saw the money, saw a skill I could learn. Started building little games in Objective-C.

**2014.** Senior year of high school. Swift gets announced. Switched immediately and never looked back.

**2017.** Shipped **The College App**, my first real thing. Yik Yak had just been pulled, so I built the version I wanted: an anonymous campus feed paired with events and polls, with the option to post as yourself. Took off at my school, then spread to a few other campuses. Eventually closed it to chase the next thing.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>2018-2019</b> &nbsp;·&nbsp; first products, then a startup</summary>

<br>

**2018.** Built **SlidePal**, my own friending/dating app. Wanted to get away from swiping, so I leaned into *sliding* (hence the name). Launched it quietly. Never really pursued it; something more exciting showed up.

**Jun 2019.** Out of college, joined a Manhattan startup as founding engineer for **Geneva**. The pitch: a *third place*. Discord for gamers, Slack for work, Geneva for everything else.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>2019-2024</b> &nbsp;·&nbsp; six years on Geneva</summary>

<br>

**'19-'24.** The entire lifecycle. Built the app from the ground up. Learned from teammates, debated, rebuilt, shipped. Full ownership, end to end. *The kind of work that ruins you for other work.*

**2023.** On the side, shipped **FunRun.ai**: write words on a map and go run them. Tossed a clip on TikTok, it went a little viral, and the downloads followed.

**2024.** Geneva **acquired by Bumble**. Diligence, compliance, the regulatory checklist. Months of paperwork.

**2024.** Shipped **Book2Life**, quietly the work I'm most invested in. An AI reading app: read your favorite books, or have them narrated in voices that don't feel synthetic. Live summaries as you read, character portraits, scenes from the current chapter, and explanations of anything that doesn't click. The reading experience I always wanted. I use it daily.

</details>

## II. Now

*What's open on my desk this week.*

- **Runvas** is FunRun relaunched, built for real this time. Shapes, drawing, words, full route art. FunRun was the prototype; Runvas is the product.
- **Book2Life** is still my daily driver, and I keep improving it.
- **egod** is a decentralized platform for spreading good. Use Dogecoin to publicly fund proposals that help the community. Still building.
- **Bumble BFF** is Geneva, integrated and relaunched under the Bumble umbrella. Millions of new hands.
- **Banter** is a Jackbox-style party game I made with two friends. Very fun, and free.
